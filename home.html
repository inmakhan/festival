Religion




Muslim Religion

Islam, one of the world's major monotheistic religions, is founded on the belief in one God, Allah, and the guidance of the Prophet Muhammad. Central to the faith are the five pillars of Islam, which serve as the core principles guiding a Muslim's life. These pillars include the declaration of faith (Shahada), prayer (Salat), almsgiving (Zakat), fasting during the month of Ramadan (Sawm), and the pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj).

Christian Religion

Christianity, one of the world's largest and most influential religions, is founded on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ. At its core, Christianity emphasizes the values of love, compassion, forgiveness, and the pursuit of spiritual salvation through faith in Christ's teachings and redemptive sacrifice.




Sikhism Religion

Hinduism, an ancient and complex faith, embodies a rich tapestry of beliefs and traditions that have shaped the spiritual landscape of India and beyond. At its core, Hinduism emphasizes the concepts of dharma (duty), karma (action), and the pursuit of spiritual liberation through various paths, including devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jnana), and disciplined action (karma yoga).

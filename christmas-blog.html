About Of Ram Navami:

The word "Ram Navami" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Deepavali," which means "row of lights." The festival is aptly named as it is characterized by the lighting of diyas (oil lamps), candles, and decorative lights, which illuminate homes, streets, and public spaces. Ram Navami holds great cultural, religious, and social significance. It is primarily celebrated to commemorate the return of Lord Rama, along with his wife Sita and brother Lakshmana, to their kingdom of Ayodhya after 14 years of exile and the victory of good over evil. This event is described in the ancient Hindu epic, the Ramayana.
